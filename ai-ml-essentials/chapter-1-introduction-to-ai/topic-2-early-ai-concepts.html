📜 Topic 2: Early AI Concepts

1. Origins of Artificial Intelligence

The idea of creating intelligent machines has fascinated humans for centuries. Ancient myths and stories described artificial beings capable of performing human-like tasks.

2. Alan Turing and Machine Intelligence

Alan Turing is considered one of the founding fathers of Artificial Intelligence. In 1950, he proposed the famous Turing Test to evaluate whether a machine can exhibit intelligent behaviour similar to humans.

3. The Turing Test

A human evaluator communicates with both a human and a machine through text. If the evaluator cannot reliably distinguish between them, the machine is said to have passed the Turing Test.

4. Dartmouth Conference (1956)

The Dartmouth Summer Research Project on Artificial Intelligence is regarded as the birth of AI as an academic field.

John McCarthy first coined the term "Artificial Intelligence" during this event.

5. Early Expectations of AI

  • Automatic theorem proving
  • Game playing systems
  • Machine translation
  • Problem solving programs

6. Challenges Faced

  • Limited computing power
  • Insufficient data
  • Lack of efficient algorithms
  • High expectations and slow progress

7. Key Takeaways

  • AI originated from attempts to mimic human intelligence.
  • Alan Turing played a significant role in AI development.
  • The Dartmouth Conference formally established AI as a field.
  • Early AI faced several technological limitations.
